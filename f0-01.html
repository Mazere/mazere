#F0-01 - Gibson ES-350 – 1950 –

The ES350 was the first cutaway electric guitar ever issued by Gibson just after WWII. Figured wood, early P90 pickups (that would eventually become THE classic single-coil unit for decades) seduced many Jazz guitarists such as Tal Farlow and Barney Kessel.

This fully original instrument in excellent condition is a great example of the impeccable Gibson quality construction in those times.

This is the second variant of this model and, as many of the 40' Gibson instrument, it does not have a Serial Number but only a Factory Order Number (Fon) :  4185-3 . According to A.R Duchossoir research, this would date this guitar in 1950.

Logs in the picture background are meant to evocate the very ‘wood’ tonal and construction of this guitar.

Here, Tim Lerch presents a similar ES350 model from 1951
