ES-350 - History

1949 Gibson catalog presenting the ES-350.


The ES-350 was the first Gibson Electric guitar with a cutaway. The first variant of this model had only one pickup with a tone and a volume control.

The second variant, introduced late 1949, was upgraded with a second pickup, a master tone control and one volume control for each pickup. The later variant would have the classical toggle switch and separate volume/tone controls for each pickups.

Between 1947 and 1956, an estimated total of 1,100  ES-350 have been built. Among those there are about 300 units of this specific variant in sunburst finish, and about 80 built during 1948/1949 years.

Lamninated maple body. Two pieces maple neck with mahogany strip

Gibson 1949 Catalog first page, showing the newly introduced ES-5. It was the top of the electric line. Basically an ES-350 with 3 pickups and fancy neck inlays and binding
